Kate Winslet is a highly respected and accomplished actress known for her diverse film roles and powerful performances. Over her career, she has appeared in numerous successful films, earning critical acclaim and commercial success. Some of her highest-grossing movies include romantic dramas, historical films, and more. Winslet is known for her versatility and talent and is considered one of the best actresses in the industry. These top 10 highest-grossing Kate Winslet movies showcase her exceptional range and talent, and demonstrate her ability to portray complex layered characters and play romantic leads.

Little Children - 2006 ($20 Million)

Via Wikimedia Commons

"Little Children" is a 2006 drama film featuring strong performances from Kate Winslet and Patrick Wilson as two parents dealing with infidelity and moral dilemmas in their relationships. The film was a commercial success, earning over $20 million at the box office, and it received critical acclaim for its depth and complexity. It explores themes of love, loyalty, and morality and was hailed as a poignant and thought-provoking film by many reviewers.

Eternal Sunshine of the Spotless Mind - 2004 ($72 Million)

Via Flickr/Logline

Eternal Sunshine of the Spotless Mind is a critically acclaimed science fiction romantic comedy-drama film directed by Michel Gondry and written by Charlie Kaufman. It stars Jim Carrey and Kate Winslet as a couple who erase each other's memories after their relationship ends, with supporting performances from Kirsten Dunst, Mark Ruffalo, Elijah Wood, and Tom Wilkinson. The film grossed over $72 million, won the Academy Award for Best Original Screenplay, and has since become a cult classic.

Revolutionary Road - 2008 ($80 Million)

Via Flickr/Arena Dame

"Revolutionary Road" is a 1950s drama film about a young couple struggling with suburban monotony, starring Kate Winslet and Leonardo DiCaprio. It was released in 2008 and was a commercial success, earning over $80 million. The couple grapples with themes of conformity, desire, and finding meaning and fulfillment in a society that values stability over personal growth.

The Reader - 2008 ($123 Million)

Via Wikimedia Commons

Released in 2008, "The Reader" is a historical drama film set in post-World War II Germany and starring Kate Winslet as a former SS guard. The film follows the guard's relationship with a younger man and explores themes of guilt, responsibility, and redemption. It was a commercial success, earning over $123 million at the box office, and Winslet won an Academy Award for Best Actress for her performance.

Steve Jobs - 2015 ($130 Million)

Via Wikimedia Commons

A biographical drama film released in 2015, "Steve Jobs" tells the story of the co-founder of Apple and follows his life and career. Michael Fassbender stars as Steve Jobs, and Kate Winslet plays a key supporting character. The film was a commercial success, earning over $130 million at the box office and it received critical acclaim, particularly for the standout performances of Winslet and Fassbender. Many reviewers praised their effective portrayals of their characters.

Contagion - 2011 ($135 Million)

Via Flickr/Sam Soon

This is a 2011 medical thriller film, starring Kate Winslet as an epidemiologist trying to contain a global pandemic. The film earned over $135 million at the box office and received praise for its realistic portrayal of a pandemic and strong cast including Laurence Fishburne, Marion Cotillard, and Jude Law.

Sense and Sensibility - 1995 ($135 Million)

Via Flickr/Past Posters

"Sense and Sensibility" is a popular period drama film that earned over $135 million at the box office and received critical acclaim for its performances and costume design. Based on Jane Austen's novel of the same name, the film follows the Dashwood sisters as they navigate love and marriage in a society that values wealth and status. Known for its strong performances and faithful adaptation of the novel, it is a must-see for fans of Austen and period drama.

The Holiday - 2006 ($205 Million)

Via Wikimedia Commons

Kate Winslet stars in the romantic comedy "The Holiday," released in 2006. The film follows two women who exchange homes during the holiday season and features a talented cast including Jude Law, Cameron Diaz, and Jack Black. "The Holiday" was a commercial success, earning over $205 million at the box office and receiving praise for its heartwarming story and lead actor chemistry. It is set in beautiful holiday destinations and is a feel-good romantic comedy.

Divergent - 2014 ($288 Million)

Via Wikimedia Commons

"Divergent" is a sci-fi action film released in 2014 that grossed over $288 million at the box office. The movie, which stars Kate Winslet as the main antagonist, is based on a bestselling novel of the same name. Set in a future society where people are divided into factions based on their personalities, the story follows Tris, a young woman who doesn't fit into any of the factions and teams up with Four, a member of the Dauntless faction, to fight for her survival and prevent the destruction of her society.

Titanic - 1997 ($2.2 Billion)

Via Wikimedia Commons

Titanic is a film about two young lovers, Rose and Jack, on board the RMS Titanic, which sinks after hitting an iceberg. The film is set in 1912 and is based on the true story of the disaster. Rose is unhappy with her arranged marriage, while Jack is a poor artist. The two fall in love and try to escape together, but must fight for survival as the ship sinks. The film was directed by James Cameron and became the highest-grossing film of all time with a worldwide box office gross of over $2.2 billion, winning 11 Academy Awards. It also had a successful soundtrack and a hit song by Celine Dion. The film remains a classic to this day.
