In just under 10 years, Jessie J has become one of the best-selling pop stars in the world, having dominated the charts with hits such as Do It Like A Dude, Mama Knows Best, and Bang Bang. She's even experienced commercial success in the US, where her song Domino peaked at number six on the Billboard Hot 100.

Before she became a household name, Jessie J (real name: Jessica Ellen Cornish) was making a living by writing songs for other mainstream artists, including Chris Brown and Miley Cyrus. For Miley, Jessie co-wrote the chart-topping hit, 'Party In The USA,' which she said paid her rent for three years.

Her net worth stands at $30 million, but how did she amass such a big fortune in such a short amount of time? Here's what you need to know.

13 She Co-Wrote Miley Cyrus' 'Party In The USA'

Many people don’t know that Miley Cyrus’ 2009 hit, ‘Party In The USA’, was co-written by Jessie J, way before she became a household name. To date, the song has sold over 7 million copies in the USA, so it’s fair to assume that Jessie got paid quite a bit for co-writing that big hit.

When Jessie J signed on to become a judge on The Voice UK in 2012, she was reportedly paid an estimated $250,000 per season - the ‘Domino’ singer would go on to appear on two seasons of the show, before she decided to leave. She left to focus on her next album.

11 ... And Later Signed Up For 'The Voice Australia'

Jessie ended up signing on for another run of the popular talent show, but this time, she was honing her judging skills on the Australian edition of The Voice. It’s unclear how much she made working in the Land Down Under, but it’s fair to assume that those numbers were probably similar to what she had earned on The Voice in the UK.

10 Her Debut Album Was A Commercial Success

Jessie J’s debut album, ‘Who You Are’, was an enormous - and unexpected - commercial success, spawning hits not only in the UK but also in the USA. Her single, ‘Domino,’ peaked at no. 6 on the Billboard Hot 100 and went on to sell over five million copies worldwide. The album, which is primarily filled with songs written by Jessie, went on to sell over six million copies worldwide.
