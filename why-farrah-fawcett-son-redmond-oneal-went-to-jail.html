Here's Why Farrah Fawcett's Son Redmond O'Neal Went To Jail

August 15, 2024
post

Farrah Fawcett passed away in 2009, and although she had a long marriage to 1970s Hollywood hunk Lee Majors and dated several others, she only had one child with her partner Ryan O'Neal. Their son Redmond O'Neal was born in 1985 and his life, like the lives of many celebrity offspring, has been a tumultuous and tragic one.

As a young man he enjoyed working as a voice actor, and he even performed in some of the most iconic children's films and shows of recent memory. But he has also endured a public arrest and caused pain and suffering to several individuals, all while struggling with an intense and violent degree of mental illness. O'Neal has been in legal trouble ever since his latest arrest in 2018, and between firing his attorney and still facing a hefty civil suit, it has not been going well for him.

6 Who Is Redmond O'Neal?

First, let us get to know the only birth child of Farrah Fawcett a little bit better. O'Neal has a long history of legal problems; in 2008 he was arrested in his Malibu home for narcotics possession, and in 2009, as his mother was dying, he got arrested for DUI and possession of narcotics. He violated probation in 2015 and was then sent to prison for three years. Then, in 2018, the worst of his problems began. But before he was Redmond O'Neal the violent criminal, he was Redmond O'Neal the voice actor and he loaned his voice to The Brave Little Toaster, Johnny Bravo, and several classics of the 90s.

5 Redmond O'Neal Was Caught Robbing A Liquor Store

After his initial release from prison, he was arrested once again, this time for robbing a convenience store in Santa Monica, California. After his arrest in Santa Monica, police soon realized that Redmond O'Neal was guilty of much more than just armed robbery, however. His sporadic and uncontrollable behavior also led them to believe that he was not of a sound state of mind and that either drugs, mental illness, or both were a factor in the case.

4 Redmond O'Neal Was Linked To Multiple Violent Crimes

Soon after his 2018 arrest, investigators began to connect the dots and realized that O'Neal was the perpetrator of several other violent crimes that had taken place in Southern California during that week. O'Neal was implicated in a week-long crime spree that included allegations of assault with a deadly weapon and attempted murder. One man alleges that O'Neal tried to stab him to death. Although the case against him was very strong, mental health professionals had to be called in.

3 Redmond O'Neal Was Deemed Unfit For Trial

After an evaluation of his mental health, Redmond O'Neal was diagnosed with bipolar disorder, schizophrenia, and antisocial personality disorder. Upon hearing this, the judge had no choice but to rule that O'Neal was unfit to stand trial and he was transferred to a mental hospital. Experts believe that his poor mental health and his heavy drug and alcohol use triggered his violent episode. O'Neal, however, does not. O'Neal spoke out from prison after finally receiving treatment, and he stated that he believes it was the drugs or alcohol that caused him to lose control, and that his parents are the ones to blame for his actions. Although he avoided a criminal trial, this did not end his legal woes, far from it as a matter of fact.

2 Redmond O'Neal Is Still In Legal Trouble

While he no longer faces criminal charges for his incredibly violent crimes, he still faces incredibly financial hardships. The judge ruled that he was unfit to stand a criminal trial, but the person who O'Neal allegedly tried to stab to death was given a thumbs up from the court to pursue a civil case against the former voice actor. To make matters even worse, the stabbing was allegedly racially motivated and therefore qualifies as a hate crime. Things got even weirder in the case when O'Neal fired his defense attorney in 2020.

1 FYI Redmond O'Neal Inherited Millions From His Mom

Wealth can either help or hurt mental illness, one can either use their money to get quality care and treatment and therefore have go on to have great careers and personal lives, or one could use the money to enable their worst habits and triggers. It is so common for celebrity children and former child actors to fall into the latter that it isn't even shocking when it happens anymore. It would appear that O'Neal went down this road and has little chance of coming back from it. The victims of his alleged hate crime are suing for nearly $100 million in damages. O'Neal only inherited $4.5 million when his mother died, and the money was held in a revocable trust, and one can probably imagine why. Mental illness and addiction can be very scary things, and the life and crimes of Redmond O'Neal are proof of that.
