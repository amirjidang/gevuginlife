Demond “Bob” Sanders (born February 24, 1981) is a retired American professional football player who spent eight seasons as a safety in the National Football League (NFL). He was a member of the University of Iowa football team. Sanders was selected in the second round of the 2004 NFL Draft by the Indianapolis Colts, and he previously played for the San Diego Chargers. He was awarded AP NFL Defensive Player of the Year in 2007 and is most remembered for being a key member of the Colts’ Super Bowl XLI-winning playoff defence. He was dubbed “The Hitman” for his strong hits and tackles, and “The Eraser” by former Colts coach Tony Dungy for his proclivity for erasing his teammates’ errors. He has, however, been renowned for his frequent ailments, having just twice played more than six games in a season, in 2005 and 2007.
