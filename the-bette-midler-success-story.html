Bette Midler has a big impact in Hollywood, and her career dates back to the 1960s when the young woman was cast as a movie extra before she enchanted audiences with her humor, energy, and dynamic acting skills. An actor with multiple accolades to her name, let’s look at the Bette Midler success story that led her to acquire $250 million.

A Career That Began On Broadway

Bette Midler was born in Oahu, Hawaii, where her mother was a seamstress while her father was a painter. She was interested in singing as a young child and, with encouragement from her mother, continued her passion. Midler performed in several local productions during her high school years before enrolling at the University of Hawaii. According to Britannica, she quit college to pursue her dream of acting and landed her first role in the 1965 movie Hawaii. She played a seasick extra passenger and moved to Hollywood with the film crew. After saving enough money from her uncredited roles, Midler moved to New York.

New York was filled with challenges for Midler, who was adamant about making her career work. She gave her first big performance in 1965 in the Experimental Theater. She played in Off-Broadway productions of Cinderella Revisited and Miss Nefertiti Regrets. Within a year, she was cast in her first Broadway production in Fiddler On The Roof in 1966 as Tzeitel. She worked in the role for three years before joining the Off-Broadway production of Salvation in 1969, as noted by Broadway World. She performed at various events and shows while simultaneously working on her music career.

Becoming The Divine Miss M

Bette Midler made her music debut in 1972 with The Divine Miss M, which had Barry Manilow’s production. The album became a massive success and went platinum in North America. Her album was nominated for Album Of The Year at the Grammys, and she received the award for Best New Artist. Midler not only released studio albums but also produced and sang for movie soundtracks. In 1988, she produced the music for her drama/comedy film Beaches, wherein the song Wind Beneath My Wings peaked at the No.1 spot on Billboard Hot 100 and remained on the charts for a whopping 29 weeks, as stated by Billboard.

Movie roles also came calling for the singer, and she ventured into acting in the late 1970s. After working in many movies as an uncredited extra, Bette Middle was cast as a lead in Mark Rydell’s The Rose in 1979. The film was a critical and commercial success, with Midler working in front of the camera and providing songs for its track. She earned an Oscar nomination and won two Golden Globe Awards: New Star Of The Year and Best Actress. Her song The Rose also won her a Grammy for Best Female Pop Vocal Performance.

A Lustrous And Inspiring Career

Bette Midler continued her work in cinema and won another Golden Globe in 1992 for her performance in For The Boys. She made an appearance on The Tonight Show, Starring Johnny Carson 25 times, and won an Emmy for her musical performance on the show. She was also Caron’s last guest when he retired in 1992. As mentioned by Screen Rant, some of her other notable performances include Parental Guidance, Stella, Big Business, The First Wives Club, Ruthless, and Hocus Pocus.

After a long hiatus from Broadway, Bette Midler made a much-anticipated return in the 2017 Broadway revival production of Hello, Dolly! as Dolly Gallagher Levi. She won a Tony Award for Best Musical Actress for her portrayal. Since the 1970s, Middle has also embarked on multiple musical tours, and her tenth tour occurred in 2015 with Divine Intervention Tour that began in America and ended in England.

Bette Midler began her career in minor roles and traveling with the film crew to various locations. She found her calling on Broadway and blossomed her career to become one of her most highly-regarded stage and movie actresses. Midler continues to work in selective projects and more recently starred in Hocus Pocus 2 alongside Sarah Jessica Parker and Kathy Najimy.
