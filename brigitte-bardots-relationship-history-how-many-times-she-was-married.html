The details of her romantic relationships, how they played out, and how she managed them show a different side of Bardot.

8 Brigitte Bardot's First Marriage Was With Roger Vadim

According to People, Brigitte Bardot was just 18 when she tied the knot with her first husband, Roger Vadim. Vadim was a film director, and Bardot was only 15 when they first met. Because of their age gap, her parents didn't like their relationship.

But they got married anyway, and Vadim started directing Bardot in a few movies. One of them was the film, And God Created Woman. This movie was a big hit, and Bardot became famous as a sex symbol.

However, their success in work didn't help their marriage. After five years, they decided to get a divorce. During the filming of And God Created Woman, Bardot fell in love with another actor, Jean-Louis Trintignant. Even though Bardot and Vadim ended their marriage, they didn't fight and separated on good terms in April 1957.

7 Brigitte Bardot's Second Marriage Was With Jacques Charrier

After her first marriage ended, Brigitte Bardot married again. This time, she wed actor Jacques Charrier in 1959. They met while making the film Babette Goes to War. The next year, Bardot became a mom when their son, Nicolas Charrier, was born.

But Bardot was having a hard time. She was famous and a new mom, and her marriage wasn't going well. It was a lot to handle. After three years, Bardot and Charrier decided to end their marriage. When they got divorced in 1962, Charrier was the one who took care of their son Nicolas.

According to Demotix, their relationship has been tense from the beginning and has remained that way due to various circumstances, primarily from Bardot's end.

5 Brigitte Bardot's Third Husband Was Gunther Sachs

Brigitte Bardot's third marriage was to Gunther Sachs. He was a wealthy German man who was known for being a playboy and a photographer. Sachs really liked Bardot and did big things to win her heart. He once sent a helicopter to drop hundreds of roses over her house.

They met in Saint-Tropez in May 1966 and got married in Las Vegas just two months later. But things didn't stay happy for long. Bardot started seeing singer Mike Sarne shortly after they returned from their wedding. There were more affairs after that, and Bardot and Sachs decided to get a divorce in 1969. Despite everything, they stayed friendly. Sachs even said later that one year with Bardot was better than ten years with anyone else.

4 Brigitte Bardot Has Had Over 100 Lovers In Her Lifetime

According to the Daily Mail, Brigitte Bardot was known for having many lovers. She had over 100 partners, some of whom were even women. Even while she was married, she had other relationships. She had an affair with Jean-Louis Trintignant while she was married to her first husband. Trintignant ended up divorcing his wife, and he and Bardot lived together for two years.

But Bardot moved on. She fell for singer Gilbert Becaud, even though he was married. This pattern of one relationship after another was typical for Bardot, and she was usually the one to end things. After Becaud, Bardot had a relationship with singer Sacha Distel. She also had flings with actor Warren Beatty, musicians Serge Gainsbourg and Nino Ferrer, co-star Mike Sarne, and Brazilian artist Bab Zagury.

3 Did Brigitte Bardot Really Try To End Her Life?

Bardot has struggled with her mental health over the years, which led her to attempt to take her life multiple times. According to Daily Mail, in a desperate moment, overwhelmed by the thought of losing her lover, she tried to take her life by inhaling gas from an oven, but her parents found her in time.

She also attempted to end her life on her 26th birthday in 1960 by overdosing on sleeping pills and harming her wrists at her home in Nice, France. These incidents were among the many tragic episodes she faced before deciding to step away from the limelight.

2 Brigitte Bardot Is Currently Married To Bernard d'Ormale

After being single for over 20 years, Brigitte Bardot married again in 1992. Her husband is Bernard d'Ormale, a businessman who used to work with the French National Front. d'Ormale prefers to stay out of the spotlight. He and Bardot met in July 1992 and were married by August, which made it a very quick courtship.

d'Ormale says that their relationship helped Bardot reconnect with her son, Nicolas. Bardot even called Nicolas to introduce him to d'Ormale. While planning a trip to see Nicolas in Norway, Bardot suggested they get married there, and they did.

1 Brigitte Bardot Was Always Fined For Racial Slurs

According to Vanity Fair, Brigitte Bardot has gotten in trouble for saying things that are racist. In an open letter, she called people from Réunion island a French territory in the Indian Ocean "degenerate savages." For this, a French court fined her 20,000 euros, which is about $23,000. This fine was for "inciting racial hatred."

Before that, Bardot had to pay a fine of 15,000 euros in 2008. That time, it was for a letter she wrote to Nicolas Sarkozy about animal killing. In that letter, she said some negative things about Muslims, which were considered harmful. According to a BBC report, she said Muslims were "destroying us, destroying our country by imposing their acts." This was the sixth time Bardot was fined for making racist comments.
