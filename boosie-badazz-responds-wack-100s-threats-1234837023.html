Boosie Badazz Responds To Wack 100's Threats

August 14, 2024
post

Boosie Badazz has responded to Wack 100‘s threats of physical assault, warning that he could potentially turn into a “maniac” if given reason.

On Thursday (Dec. 28), Boosie hopped on Instagram Live and assured his followers that he was unconcerned with Wack 100’s words, distancing himself from “opps” while insisting he’s comfortable in his lifestyle and in no rush to beef.

“I ain’t worried about nobody,” the 41-year-old said. “I don’t even go no opps. I don’t even go no opps no more. I’m in a city where everybody love me. Well, not everybody. I mean, I don’t got no problem nowhere I go. I ain’t in no beef with no ni**a. Life is great.”

However, the rapper made it clear that when presented with threats or danger, he’s not one to cower and that the outcome could be bleak for those attempting to cause him harm. “When I got opps, it’s bad for a lot of people,” he said. “It’s not gonna be a good day… Ni**a don’t want to turn me into no maniac.”

Boosie and Wack 100 have become at odds following Wack 100’s claims that rapper B.G. cooperated with law enforcement and “snitched” prior to being released from prison. Those comments led Boosie, as well as Cash Money Records founder Birdman, to lash out at the rap manager for attempting to smear their fellow Louisiana native’s name.

In response, Wack 100 recalled Boosie labeling others informants based off legal documents before threatening to “beat” the hitmaker up. “I will beat the f**k out of you, Boosie,” the Los Angeles native said. “You know me in real life. You weigh about 117 pounds. I will beat the dog sh*t out of you, boy. I will catch you.”
