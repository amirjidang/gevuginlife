American actor Anthony Dwane Mackie was born in New Orleans, Louisiana, on September 23, 1978. With the release of the 2002 film 8 Mile, which was based on the life of rapper Eminem, he began his acting career.

Later, he made appearances in a number of films and was even nominated for the Independent Spirit Award for Best Actor for his performance in the LGBT-themed drama Brother to Brother. He appeared in The Manchurian Candidate and Million Dollar Baby both in the same year.

Two years after appearing in the critically acclaimed film Half Nelson in 2006, Mackie was nominated for an Independent Spirit Award for Best Supporting Actor for his work in The Hurt Locker.

Along with his career in movies, Mackie additionally played Tupac Shakur in the 2009 film Notorious. Additionally, he appeared in the 2010 and 2011 films Night Catches Us, The Adjustment Bureau, and Real Steel.

Martha and Willie Mackie Sr. are Mackie’s parents. His brother Calvin Mackie is a former associate professor of engineering at Tulane University, and his father was a carpenter who had a roofing company called Mackie Roofing.
