Early Life & Biography

Jason John Bonham was born on 15 July 1966 in Dudley, West Midlands, England. He is the son of the late Led Zeppelin drummer John Bonham and Patricia “Pat” Bonham (née Phillips). At the age of five, he could play drums very well and also appeared in the movie The Song Remains the Same along with his father.

When Bonham was fifteen years old, he joined the rock band Airrace. He was also the part of the British rock band Virginia Woolf, with whom he made two music albums.

Personal Life

Bonham married Jan Charteris in Stone, Kidderminster, in 1990. At his wedding reception, he performed a jam with popular artists Jimmy Page, Robert Plant, and John Paul Jones. The couple has two children, a son named Jager and a daughter, Jaz.

Career

Jason Bonham started his professional music career in 1988 when he joined former Led Zeppelin guitarist Jimmy Page for his Outrider album and tour. In the same year, he performed at Atlantic Records 40th Anniversary concert in New York City.

In 1989, along with several other artists, he performed the single “Rock and Roll” at the Moscow Music Peace Festival. In the same year, he formed his own music group “Bonham” and released the debut single The Disregard of Timekeeping, which was an instant hit.

Bonham played drums at the 1990 Grammy Awards for Paul Rodgers. The following year, with Rodgers and Slash, he performed at the Woodstock II music festival. In 1994, he renamed his band as Motherland and released the album Peace 4 Me.

In 1997, he recorded the music album “In the Name of My Father – The Zepset,” which featured the songs of his father and the rock band Led Zeppelin. Bonham was invited to drum for hard rock group UFO.

Jason Bonham also appeared in the 2001 movie Rock Star in which he played the role of drummer A.C. The movie also featured performances by artists such as Jeff Pilson, Brian Vander Ark, Ralph Michael Saenz, among others.

Bonham also appeared in the 2006 reality television show Supergroup along with Ted Nugent, Evan Seinfeld, Sebastian Bach, and Scott Ian, which aired on the VH1 network. All the artists lived in a mansion in Las Vegas for twelve days and created music.

From 2004 to 2007, Bonham also played drums for the British-American rock band, Foreigner. He was also part of the music group Black Country Communion until they broke up in 2013.

At the 2007 Led Zeppelin reunion concert, Bonham stepped into his father’s shoes and played drums, and his performance was described as “flawless” by music critics. Along with his sisters Ann and Nancy Wilson, he played drums for the rock band “Heart.”

In 2012, Bonham performed the Led Zeppelin tribute at the Kennedy Center Honors, which was hosted by President Barack Obama. The following year, he teamed up again with Glenn Hughes to form a new rock band, California Breed, adding the guitarist Andrew Watt.
