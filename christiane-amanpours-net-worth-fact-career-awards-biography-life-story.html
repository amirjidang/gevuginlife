Christiane Amanpour is a British-Iranian journalist and television anchor who is well-known around the world for her bravery in covering numerous wars from some of the most dangerous places and hosting various shows on news channels like ABC, PBS, and CNN. For her work, she won numerous honorary degrees and accolades, including Peabody Awards and an Edward R. Murrow Award.

After starting her career as an entry-level desk assistant at CNN, Amanpour’s coverage of the Persian Gulf War in 1990 gained her widespread attention, as well as elevating CNN’s news coverage to new heights. In 1983, Christiane Amanpour began her journalism career at CNN. She was assigned to cover numerous wars and revolutions as a correspondent. Her first significant assignment was to cover the Iran-Iraq war, which was followed by a report on the downfall of communism in Europe. 

Her other notable works included coverage of the Eastern European democratic movements, the Persian Gulf War, and the Bosnian war.

Where is Christiane Amanpour from?

Christiane Amanpour was born on 12 January 1958 in Ealing, Middlesex, England. Her birth name is Christiane Maria Heideh Amanpour. Her nationality is British. Amanpour belongs to mixed ethnicity and her zodiac sign is Capricorn.

Born as the eldest of four daughters to her English mother Patricia Anne Amanpour and her Iranian father Mohammad Amanpour, she lived in Tehran, Iran with her sisters until her late teens/the early twenties. As a child, she grew up in an affluent environment. 

When she was 11 years old, her parents sent her to Holy Cross Convent, an all-girls school in Chalfont St Peter, Buckinghamshire in England. She relocated to New Hall, England’s oldest girls’ Catholic school when she was sixteen years old. After the Islamic Revolution occurred in January 1979, her family was forced to flee the country, losing everything they had in Iran. This experience helped Christiane decide on Journalism as a career to pursue.

Later Amanpour went to the University of Rhode Island to get a degree in journalism where she also got the chance to work in the news department at WBRU-FM in Providence, Rhode Island. She also worked as an electronics graphics designer for NCB affiliate WJAR in Providence. Amanpour earned a B.A. in journalism from the institution summa cum laude and Phi Beta Kappa in 1983.
