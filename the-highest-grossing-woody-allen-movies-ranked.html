Woody Allen is an American screenwriter, actor, and director. He is well-known for his outstanding career in the film business, where he has directed, written, and sometimes featured in a few films. Throughout Allen's career, he has been recognized for his distinctive style, blending comedy and drama with a focus on neurotic characters and intellectual humor. His films often explore love, relationships, morality, and existentialism themes. Allen's work frequently reflects his experiences and interests, drawing inspiration from literature, philosophy, and his love for New York City. Woody Allen has written, directed, or acted in over 80 films, which has enabled Allen to amass his $140 million fortune.

Midnight in Paris -$154 Million

Midnight in Paris is a romantic comedy-fantasy film written and directed by Woody Allen. It was released in 2011 and received critical acclaim, becoming one of Allen's most successful and beloved films. It follows the story of Gil Pender, played by Owen Wilson, a disillusioned Hollywood screenwriter on a trip to Paris with his fiancée and her family. The film explores nostalgia, creative desire and the charm of the past. The film was a commercial success, generating $154 million against a $17 million budget.

Blue Jasmine -$99 Million

Blue Jasmine is a drama film written and directed by Woody Allen, released in 2013. It follows Jasmine French, played by Cate Blanchett, a once-wealthy New York socialite who falls from success after her husband, Alec Baldwin, is revealed as a scammer, and they lose their entire wealth. The film received critical acclaim and numerous accolades.

Vicky Cristina Barcelona -$96 Million

Vicky Cristina Barcelona is a romantic comedy-drama film written and directed by Woody Allen. This film explores themes of love, passion, and self-discovery. Vicky Cristina Barcelona showcases Woody Allen's signature style of mixing comedy and drama, focusing on complex relationships and moral dilemmas. The movie was released in 2008 and received positive reviews from critics. The film was a commercial success, generating $96 million against a $15 million budget.

Match Point -$85 Million

Match Point is a psychological thriller written and directed by Woody Allen. The story follows Chris Wilton, played by Jonathan Rhys Meyers, a former professional tennis player from Ireland who becomes a tennis instructor in London. He befriends Tom Hewett, portrayed by Matthew Goode, a wealthy young man who introduces Chris to his family. The movie's success improved Allen's career and earned him critical compliments. Match Point movie was critically acclaimed and is widely recognized as one of Allen's most outstanding efforts. It grossed $85 million against a budget of $15 million.

Hannah and Her Sisters -$40 Million

Hannah and Her Sisters is a comedy-drama film written and directed by Woody Allen. It was released in 1986 and is considered one of Allen's most critically acclaimed and successful works. The film follows three sisters, Hannah (Mia Farrow), Lee (Barbara Hershey), and Holly Dianne Wiest, who have complicated relationships, love entanglements, and emotional challenges. It also follows the lives of the two men connected to the sisters, including Hannah's husband Elliot (Michael Caine) and Lee's partner Frederick (Max von Sydow). The film digs deeply into love, betrayal, and family relationships.

Manhattan -$39 Million

Manhattan follows the complex love life of Isaac Davis (Woody Allen), a neurotic comedy writer dating a teenage girl, Tracy (Mariel Hemingway), while also dating his best friend's mistress. The film delves into subjects such as love, relationships, and the intricacies of human desires. It is regarded as one of Allen's most significant works and a classic example of his distinct filming technique.

Scoop  -$39 Million

It was released in 2006 and featured a blend of humor and suspense, characteristic of Allen's style. The story follows Sondra Pransky, played by Scarlett Johansson, an American journalism student studying in London. While attending a magic show, Sondra is contacted by the spirit of a deceased journalist who reveals the identity of a notorious serial killer. With the help of a charming magician named Sid Waterman, played by Woody Allen, Sondra embarks on an investigative journey to expose the killer.

Annie Hall -$38 Million

It is often regarded as one of Allen's greatest and most influential works, earning widespread critical acclaim and winning four Academy Awards, including Best Picture. The film follows the relationship between Alvy Singer, played by Woody Allen, a neurotic New York comedian, and Annie Hall, portrayed by Diane Keaton, an aspiring singer. The film covers the ups and downs of their relationship, their unique characteristics, and the obstacles they confront as individuals using various non-linear narrative methods such as flashbacks and breaching the fourth wall.
