Britt Robertson's Acting Roles

While KJ Apa's girlfriend is pregnant, the Riverdale star was once romantically linked to Britt Robertson, according to Elle.com.

While Britt Robertson is a familiar name to movie fans, she has never headlined a major Hollywood blockbuster or starred on a TV show that went on for more than one season. Britt talked about her acting jobs with Buzzfeed News and the actress said, "I've had such a weird career. I've kind of done little bits of everything."

Britt Robertson explained to Buzzfeed News that some of her roles weren't all that meaty, and that in other cases, she was so tired from filming that she might not have done the best acting job that she would now.

Britt took on the starring role of Cassie Blake in the CW drama The Secret Circle, which aired for one season in 2011. Cassie found out that she was a witch when she relocated to a town called Chance Harbor, and she became part of a coven. While that sounds like a thrilling and fascinating premise for a TV show, it was canceled after only one season.

Britt talked about it with Buzzfeed News and said, "It's a similar thing with Scream 4; I wish I would have made something out of nothing." She said the filming was "exhausting" and explained, "there were so many days where I would just be exhausted and not feeling like I had enough in me or, like, OK, well, I don't know what to do with this, so I'm going to do nothing with it."

About her small role in Scream 4 in the opening sequence, Britt said, "I loved the Scream movies, and it's the only reason I did it. I was like, OK, there's no character here. I have no idea what I'm saying. This makes no sense. I don't know what to do. And I don't necessarily regret that experience. I just wish I would have taken advantage of it more."

Britt Appeared In 'Tomorrowland' And 'Ask Me Anything'

Britt Robertson starred as Casey Newton in the 2015 movie Tomorowland, and according to Page Six, Britt didn't promote her movie Ask Me Anything. Allison Burnett, the director of Ask Me Anything, said that Britt loved being in the movie and said it was an amazing experience: “when the movie was finished, she wrote to me and said she’d never been more proud of anything in her career and she’d do anything to help promote it.”

Britt Robertson's Many TV Roles

Some of Britt Roberton's other roles include Angie McAlister in the TV adaptation of Stephen King's novel Under The Dome, which only got one season, and Lux Cassidy on the single-season TV show Life Unexpected.

If these shows had lasted for longer, then maybe Britt would be a bit of a bigger name, but she did show her acting talents in each one.

On Life Unexpected, Lux finds her birth parents, Cate and Nate. She wants to leave the foster care system and get emancipated and they end up taking care of her. It's just as heartwarming as it sounds.

In an interview with PopSugar, Britt talkeda bout how much she loves the characters on the show. Britt said, "One of the great things about the show is that our characters are really real, and they're different and they're put in these extraordinary circumstances. It's really cool; it's a really great show you want to sort of dive into, and really feel for these characters. You want them to explore all these relationships and explore all these emotions, and you want to go on this ride with them, and I think that's the great appeal for our show."

While Britt Robertson might not necessarily be a household name, she has been acting a lot over the years, and according to IMDb, she just finished the movie A Mouthful Of Air and is currently filming The Re-Education Of Molly Singer, so fans will see more from her.
