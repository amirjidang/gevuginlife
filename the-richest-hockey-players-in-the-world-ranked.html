Hockey is a fast-paced team sport with players using sticks to shoot a hard rubber disc called a puck into the opposing team's goal to score points. The game involves teams of 11 players, including a goalkeeper, and the players use sticks with a curved end to control and strike the ball into the opposing team's goal. It is known for its high speed, physicality, skillful stick-handling, and teamwork. It is played at various levels, including recreational, amateur, and professional leagues. Hockey is popular in many countries worldwide, including Canada, the United States, Russia, Sweden, Finland, and many more, and it has a rich history and a passionate fan base. There are numerous hockey players worldwide, ranging from professional leagues like the National Hockey League (NHL) to international competitions.

Wayne Gretzky -$250 million

Wayne Gretzky is recognized mainly as the best player in hockey history. Born on January 1961 in Brantford, Ontario, Canada, Gretzky had an illustrious career for 21 years. He played primarily as a center and achieved remarkable success throughout his career. He set numerous records and earned numerous accolades, including winning the NHL's most valuable player award and the Hart Trophy, with a record nine times. He elevated the sport to new heights and became a cultural icon. After his retirement as a player, Gretzky also served as a coach and executive for various teams.

Mario Lemieux -$200 million

Mario Lemieux, born in October 1965 in Quebec, Canada, is considered one of the greatest hockey players ever. Lemieux played primarily as a center and had a remarkable career from 1984 to 2006. Lemieux played most of his career with the Pittsburgh Penguins in the NHL. He is known for his exceptional skill, size, and scoring ability and has become a dominant force in the league. Despite battling various health issues, including cancer, Lemieux achieved numerous accolades and set multiple records.

Alexander Ovechkin -$80 million

Alexander Ovechkin, often called Ovi, is a Russian professional ice hockey player born on September 1985 in Moscow, Russia. He is widely regarded as one of the greatest goal scorers in the history of the Hockey sport. Ovechkin has played most of his career with the Washington Capitals in the NHL). The Capitals selected him with the first overall pick in the 2004 NHL Draft. When he entered the league, Ovechkin made an immediate impact and quickly established himself as an offensive powerhouse.

Pavel Bure -$70 million

Pavel Bure, often called The Russian Rocket, is a retired Russian professional ice hockey player. He was born on March 1971 in Moscow. Bure is widely regarded as one of the most electrifying and dynamic players in the sport's history. Bure played in the National Hockey League (NHL) for the Vancouver Canucks, Florida Panthers, and New York Rangers. He immediately impacted the league, winning the Calder Memorial Trophy as the NHL's Rookie of the Year in the 1991-1992 seasons.

Chris Pronger -$70 million

Chris Pronger is a former Canadian professional ice hockey player born in October 1974 in Dryden, Ontario, Canada. He is regarded as one of the greatest defense-men in the sport's history. Pronger had a successful career in the National Hockey League (NHL), playing for several teams, including the Hartford Whalers/Carolina Hurricanes, St. Louis Blues, Edmonton Oilers, Anaheim Ducks, and Philadelphia Flyers. Standing 6 feet 6 inches (198 cm) tall, Pronger was known for imposing physical presence on the ice.

Joe Sakic -$65 million

Before the team relocated, Sakic played his entire National Hockey League (NHL) career with the Colorado Avalanche, formerly the Quebec Nordiques. He was known for his exceptional skill, leadership, and consistency throughout his career. Sakic achieved numerous individual and team successes during his time in the NHL. Sakic won the Hart Trophy as the NHL's most valuable player in 2001 and was named to the NHL All-Star Team numerous times. Jarome Iginla -$60 million

Jarome Iginla is a retired Canadian professional ice hockey player born on July 1, 1977, in Edmonton, Alberta, Canada. He is considered one of the most incredible power forwards in the sport's history. Iginla had a remarkable career in the National Hockey League (NHL), playing for several teams, most notably the Calgary Flames. He was known for his physicality, goal-scoring ability, and leadership on and off the ice. Iginla achieved numerous accolades and individual successes during his time in the NHL. Joe Thornton -$60 million
