Career

After graduating from college, Tur got a job at KTLA, a CW-affiliated channel based in Los Angeles. She worked at the channel's assignment desk for about a year before moving to HD News/Cablevision, where she worked as a producer from 2006 to 2007.

After Cablevision, Tur got another job at News 12 Brooklyn, where she worked as a reporter covering the Bronx and Brooklyn, New York. Later, she would work at WPIX-TV and Fox 5 New York as a reporter.

In 2009, Tur joined the Weather Channel, working as a storm chaser for the station’s Vortex2 team. However, the gig was short-lived, as she later joined WNBC-TV, NBC's flagship station based in New York City.

After working at WNBC-TV for a while, Tur was promoted to the network's national platform in 2012. She has worked for several of the network's news programs such as MSNBC, NBC Nightly News, The Today Show, Early Today, Meet the Press and others.

Tur has also gained a reputation for excellent news reporting due to her coverage of various breaking news events. For example, she covered the March 2008 crane collapse in Manhattan, for which she won an AP's Best Spot News Award.

Other famous coverages include the search of Malaysian Airlines Flight 370, an SUV-Biker gang road rage incident, and the death of Canadian actor Corey Monteith.

Another highlight in Katy Tur's career was the coverage of the 2016 US elections. She was embedded in the Donald Trump presidential campaign during the coverage. She covered the campaign trail, conducted interviews with Trump, and informed his campaign team about the Access Hollywood tape.

Unfortunately, Tur's time on Trump's campaign was not smooth sailing. Trump accused several news organisations of being “Fake News” and singled her out during his campaign rallies. She was booed and verbally harassed by Trump's supporters following the incident.
